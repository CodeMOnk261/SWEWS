1. Project Overview
Problem Statement
Modern communication, navigation, defense, and weather satellites operate in Geostationary Earth Orbit
(GEO), where they are continuously exposed to energetic electrons trapped within Earth's magnetosphere.
During solar storms, electron ﬂux can increase rapidly, causing:
Surface charging
Deep dielectric charging
Single Event Upsets (SEUs)
Solar panel degradation
Communication failures
Satellite subsystem damage
The objective of this project is to develop an Artiﬁcial Intelligence based Space Weather Early Warning
System capable of predicting energetic electron ﬂuxes before hazardous radiation events occur .
The system should provide reliable forecasts for:
30 minutes ahead
45 minutes ahead
6 hours ahead
12 hours ahead
Unlike traditional forecasting methods, the proposed system combines physics-based space weather
observations with modern Transformer-based deep learning models and a hybrid prediction strategy.

interactive dashboard and API suitable for research, demonstration, and operational decision support.
